Call for External Mentor

HKBU JOUR2106 Data Visualisation for News is calling for external mentors to work with the students on course projects.

The Mentorship Program brings small groups of 4 to 6 students together with one mentor to do a final project. The final delivery of course project is a functioning website with interactive storytelling or data visualization. Mentors we invited are experienced professionals/ senior executives in different industries who have a desire to help young people by sharing their knowledge and skills.

As a mentor, you are expected to bring your topic or areas of interest, and it would be even better if you could provide students with some raw data sets for analysis and visualization. After groups assigned, a kick-off meeting will be soonly arranged in which mentors could have brainstorming session with your students to come up with an appropriate topic and approach for the group project. Mentors will provide background knowledge, general advice and feedback to students, guide the students through the process of the whole project.

About the course

The course mainly focuses on data visualisation although it covers basic data analytics and data journalism workflow just to be self-contained. Among many visualisation techniques and tools, this course focuses on the web stack, because most content are distributed via (mobile) web-based technology at present. More detailed course information could be reached at: https://hkbu-jour2106.github.io/

About mentorship

Being a mentor is rewarding. You not only have fun with the students but also work with the dedicated teaching team to introduce latest web-based visualisation technology into your project. Having access to our talent pool way before summer can also ensure you an advantageous position if you are looking for new team members.

The tentative program timeline is as follows:

  • Before Mar 10: Mentor confirmation; group formation;
  • Mar 11 - April 29: Project span
    • Kick-off and brainstorming
    • Two mid-term check-points
  • April 29: Project presentation

Contact

If you or your group is interested, please email e@hupili.net. We'll be in touch to work out a mentoring plan with you. We are interested to know the following information:

  • Your organisation;
  • The problem you are working on;
  • The domain knowledge of your team, not limited to data or technology;
  • The data set you have;
  • The visualisation efforts you already tried.

Q & A

Q: What is the expected time for mentors to commit to this program?
A: The program spans 5-6 weeks. Mentors are expected to commit 1-2 hours per week discussing with the students, either offline or online, to guide them through the practical problem that we pick together.

Q: What is the copyright of any outcome?
A: The outcome will be shared authorship between students and mentors. By default, Copyright is CC 3.0 Attribution and the final product will be open source. Any referred prior works/ cited data sets remain their original license. Detailed arrangements can be discussed subject to the mutual agreement between mentors and students.

We are also ready to answer anything you'd like to know. Just shoot us an email to kick off.
